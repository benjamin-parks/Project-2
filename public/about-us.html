About Us

The shelter began as a humble endeavor by a group of animal lovers led by Rebecca Thompson, who, in 2008, converted a small warehouse into a haven for abandoned pets.

Since its inception, the shelter has facilitated the adoption of over 500 animals annually, each finding loving forever homes.

Beyond adoption services, the shelter serves as a pillar of support within the community, offering vital educational outreach programs on responsible pet ownership, including the importance of vaccinations, spaying, and neutering. These initiatives not only benefit the animals in their care but also contribute to the overall well-being of the community by fostering a culture of compassion and responsibility towards pets.

Central to their success is a robust volunteer program, welcoming individuals who dedicate their time to various tasks ranging from animal care and socialization to administrative support.

At the heart of the shelter's success is its vibrant volunteer program, which welcomes individuals from all walks of life to contribute their time and skills. Volunteers like myself are integral to the day-to-day operations, from providing hands-on care and enrichment activities for the animals to assisting with fundraising events and administrative tasks. Through the collective efforts of volunteers, staff, and supporters, the shelter continues to serve as a beacon of hope and compassion, enriching the lives of both animals and humans alike.

Central to sustaining the shelter's vital work are ongoing financial contributions and donations of supplies. Operating costs for veterinary care, food, and facility maintenance can quickly accumulate, highlighting the critical need for continued community support. Every dollar donated directly impacts the shelter's ability to provide quality care for its residents and expand its outreach efforts.

Additionally, donations of essential supplies such as bedding, food, toys, and cleaning supplies are always appreciated and help ensure the comfort and well-being of the animals in their care. With the generous support of donors and volunteers alike, the shelter can continue its mission of rescuing, rehabilitating, and rehoming animals in need, enriching the lives of both pets and people in the process.
